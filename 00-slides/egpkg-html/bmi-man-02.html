 3 Body Mass Index
 4 
 5 Description:
 6 
 7      Given weight and height, return the body mass index
 8 
 9 Usage:
10 
11      bmi(height, weight, hunits, wunits)
12 
13 Arguments:
14 
15   height: height of the subject(s)
16 
17   weight: weight of the subject(s)
18 
19   hunits: a character string for the measurement unit of height, see
20           details.
21 
22   wunits: a character string for the measurement unit of weight, see
23           details.
24 
25 Details:
26 
27      The units supported for height are:
28 
29      "in" for inches
30 
31      "cm" for centemeters
32 
33      "feet" for feet, a decimal number, e.g., 5.5 for five and one half
34           feet, or five foot six inches.
35 
36      "m" for meters
37 
38      The units supported for weight are:
39 
40      "lbs" for pounds
41 
42      "kg" for kilograms
43 
44 Value:
45 
46      a numeric vector of bmi
47 
48 Examples:
49 
50      # The bmi of a six foot one inch tall, three hundred pound individual is
51      bmi(height = 6 * 12 + 1, weight = 300, hunits = "in", wunits = "lbs")
